Collection, Segregation & Transportation

Proper collection, segregation, and transportation of waste are crucial steps in the waste management process. These steps ensure that waste is handled safely and efficiently, reducing environmental impact and improving recycling rates.

Collection

  • Waste is collected from households, businesses, and public spaces by municipal workers or private agencies.
  • Regular collection schedules help prevent littering and illegal dumping.

Segregation

  • Waste should be separated at the source into categories such as biodegradable, recyclable, and hazardous.
  • Segregation makes recycling and safe disposal easier and more effective.

Transportation

  • Collected waste is transported in covered vehicles to prevent spillage and pollution.
  • Different types of waste may require different handling and transportation methods.

By following these steps, communities can ensure cleaner surroundings and contribute to a sustainable future.
