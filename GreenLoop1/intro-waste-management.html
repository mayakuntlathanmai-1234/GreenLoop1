Introduction to Waste Management

Waste management refers to the collection, transportation, processing, recycling, and disposal of waste materials. Its main goal is to reduce the adverse effects of waste on human health, the environment, and aesthetics. Effective waste management helps conserve resources, reduce pollution, and promote sustainable development.

Why is Waste Management Important?

  • Protects public health and the environment
  • Reduces pollution and greenhouse gas emissions
  • Conserves natural resources through recycling and reuse
  • Promotes cleaner and greener communities

By understanding the basics of waste management, individuals and communities can make informed decisions to minimize waste and its impact.
